Differential Expression Analysis: Identifying PROM1 (CD133) as a Surface Target in Hepatocellular Carcinoma

Isha Chhikara | January 2025

Dataset

GSE76427 — 115 patients, HCC tumours vs adjacent healthy liver tissue. Illumina HumanHT-12 V4.0 microarray platform (GEO/NCBI).

Methods

Analysis performed in R using the Limma package (Bioconductor). Differential expression was computed with empirical Bayes moderated t-statistics. Multiple testing correction via Benjamini-Hochberg FDR. Significance thresholds: |logFC| > 0.5 and adj.P.Val < 0.05.

Key Results

logFC = 0.57 PROM1 upregulated in tumour
FDR = 0.0013 t-statistic = 3.68

Findings

  • PROM1 (CD133) identified as a significant surface target: logFC = 0.57, FDR = 0.0013, t = 3.68
  • CD133 is a known cancer stem cell marker — validates therapeutic potential for ADCs and CAR-T approaches
  • Consistent upregulation across the patient cohort confirmed via boxplot analysis
Figure 1. Volcano plot of DEA results. Red: significantly upregulated genes. PROM1 highlighted.
Figure 2. PROM1 expression: Non-Tumour (blue) vs Tumour (red), confirming consistent overexpression.

Limitations

  • Single dataset — no independent validation cohort (e.g., TCGA-LIHC RNA-seq) was used for cross-validation.
  • Microarray platform; RNA-seq would provide more granular expression data and isoform-level resolution.
