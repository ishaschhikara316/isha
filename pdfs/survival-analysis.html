Kaplan-Meier Survival Analysis of HIF1A Expression in Hepatocellular Carcinoma

Isha Chhikara | January 2025

Dataset

TCGA-LIHC (PanCancer Atlas) via cBioPortal. Clinical and mRNA expression data for Liver Hepatocellular Carcinoma patients, stratified by HIF1A expression level.

Methods

Survival modelling performed in Python 3.14 using Pandas and the Lifelines library. Patients were divided into High and Low HIF1A expression groups via median-split stratification. Overall survival was compared using the Kaplan-Meier estimator with a Log-rank test for statistical significance.

Key Result

p = 0.0549 Log-rank test (borderline significance)
Low HIF1A Associated with longer overall survival

Findings

  • Log-rank p = 0.0549 — borderline statistical significance suggesting a real biological signal
  • Low HIF1A expression associated with longer overall survival in HCC patients
  • Supports HIF1A as a prognostic marker for hepatocellular carcinoma
  • Median survival difference visible between expression groups, with clear curve separation
Figure 1. Kaplan-Meier survival curves for High vs Low HIF1A expression (TCGA-LIHC).

Limitations

  • Borderline significance (p = 0.0549) — a larger cohort is needed for definitive validation of the prognostic association.
  • Median-split stratification is simplistic; quartile-based or continuous Cox regression analysis was not performed.
